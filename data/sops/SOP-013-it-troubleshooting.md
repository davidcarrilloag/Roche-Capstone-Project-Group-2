# Troubleshooting Common IT Issues & Opening a Support Ticket

## 1. Quick Self-Help (try first)

| Problem | First steps |
|---------|-------------|
| **Printer won't print** | Check it's powered and not out of paper/toner; confirm you selected the right printer; re-send the job; if offline, restart the printer. |
| **Scanner not connecting** | Reseat the USB/network cable; restart the scanner; confirm the workstation recognises the device. |
| **Software won't open / no access** | Confirm your access was approved in the **Unified Access Portal (UAP)**; sign out and back in; clear the app and relaunch. |
| **Device login / MFA fails** | Check date/time on the workstation; re-approve the prompt in **Roche Authenticator**; ensure your badge is registered. |
| **Virtual session crashes / disconnects** | Reconnect (don't log off); note how often and which app was open. |
| **Forgot password / account locked** | Use the self-service reset, or contact the Service Desk. |

## 2. If It's Still Broken — Open a Ticket

Open an IT incident through the **Scientist Assistant** (it files the ServiceNow
ticket for you) or via the **Service Portal**:

- **Chat:** `it-helpdesk.basel-campus.internal`
- **Phone:** extension `*4433`
- **Hardware (cables, monitors, headsets):** the **IT Tech Bar**, ground floor of
  the Primary Lab Block (09:00–12:00 and 13:00–16:00).

**Include in the ticket:** what happened, when it started, the device/application
name, the exact error message, and your room / lab bay number. Good detail = faster
resolution.

## 3. How Priority Is Set

Priority is the combination of **impact** (how many people/experiments affected)
and **urgency** (how fast a fix is needed):

| Priority | Example |
|----------|---------|
| **P1 – Critical** | Lab-wide outage; a shared system down across the floor |
| **P2 – High** | An instrument PC down that blocks an active experiment |
| **P3 – Medium** | A single application issue with a workaround available |
| **P4 – Low** | Single printer down, cosmetic issue, non-urgent request |

The Service Desk logs, categorises and prioritises every incident. You'll receive
the ticket number (e.g. `INC0012345`) and can track its status from the chat.
