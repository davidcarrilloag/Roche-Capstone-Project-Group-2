# General Laboratory Equipment Cleaning & Disinfection

Covers shared bench instruments. For HP laptops, touchscreens and Engage devices
see the dedicated **Approved Disinfecting Wipes for Select HP Platforms** guide.

## 1. General Rules & PPE

- Wear gloves, lab coat and safety glasses. Power off and unplug equipment before
  cleaning unless the procedure states otherwise.
- **Standard disinfectant: 70% isopropanol (IPA).** Alcohol is most effective
  between 60–90%; 70% is the recommended working concentration.
- **Observe contact time:** keep the surface visibly **wet for 10 minutes**. If it
  dries sooner, reapply.
- Never spray liquid into electronics; apply disinfectant to a lint-free wipe.
- Record completed cleaning in the equipment logbook / **BioLIMS v4**.

## 2. Centrifuge

1. Power off, unplug, wait until the rotor has fully stopped. Remove the rotor and
   buckets.
2. **Inspect the rotor for cracks or corrosion — do not use a damaged rotor.**
3. Wipe the chamber, rotor and buckets with **70% IPA**; air-dry fully.
4. For biological spills, run the decontamination in Section 5 **before** routine
   cleaning. Leave the lid open 10 minutes to vent residual vapour.

## 3. Pipettes

1. Wipe the body with a 70% IPA wipe. **Never immerse a whole pipette in liquid.**
2. Disassemble the lower shaft **only per the manufacturer's instructions** and
   wipe with 70% IPA.
3. Check the seal/O-ring; if pipetting drifts, route the unit for service and
   recalibration (see *Equipment Maintenance & Support*).

## 4. Microscope

1. Blow loose dust off lenses with a rubber blower — **never wipe dry dust**, it
   scratches the coating.
2. Clean objective and eyepiece lenses with **lens tissue + lens-cleaning solution**
   — **not** IPA or bleach, which can damage lens cement and coatings.
3. Wipe the stage and body with a 70% IPA wipe; cover when not in use.

## 5. Decontamination After Biological Samples

1. Apply an approved disinfectant (e.g. freshly prepared 1:10 bleach, or an
   approved quaternary-ammonium product) and observe the label **contact time
   (typically 10 minutes).**
2. **Bleach is corrosive** to stainless steel and internal pipette parts — after
   the contact time, remove residue with water and finish with **70% IPA**.
3. Dispose of materials as biohazard waste per the **Laboratory Waste Management
   Guide** (Tier L1–L3).

## 6. Frequency

| Equipment | Routine clean | Deep clean |
|-----------|---------------|------------|
| Centrifuge | After each use | Weekly |
| Pipettes | Daily (exterior) | Monthly service |
| Microscope | After each session | Monthly |
| Shared benches | After each use | Daily |
