# Cold Storage Samples — Request, Transport & Handling

## 1. Storage Classes & Temperatures

| Class | Temperature | Typical use |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Room temperature | 15–25 °C | Stable reagents |
| Refrigerated | 2–8 °C | Many biological reagents, kits |
| Frozen | −20 °C | Short-term sample storage |
| Ultra-low | −80 °C (−60 to −80) | Long-term biologics, cell therapies |
| Cryogenic | ≤ −150 °C, vapour-phase liquid nitrogen | Cell lines, critical long-term samples |

## 2. Requesting Cold Samples & Reagents

- **Refrigerated/frozen reagents:** order through **ChemVantage**; ambient and
  standard chilled (+4 °C) items arrive via the automated conveyor to your Floor
  Logistics Zone.
- **Deep-frozen compounds (−30 °C to −75 °C), specialised matrices and cryogenic
  items cannot use the conveyor** — collect them **in person** at the **Central
  Supply Counter** (Main Laboratory Wing, Mon–Fri 07:30–11:00 and 12:30–16:15).
- **Biological cryo-stocks** are issued from the **First Floor Cryo-Depot**.

## 3. Transport

- Move cold-chain samples quickly; **do not exceed the time-out-of-storage** stated
  on the sample record.
- Use a **pre-cooled transport container** (dry ice for −80 °C; dry shipper for
  cryogenic). Label with content, owner and temperature class.
- **Liquid nitrogen handling:** use the **buddy system**, wear cryo-gloves, face
  shield and closed shoes; use **tongs** to remove vials. Work in a ventilated
  area (asphyxiation risk).
- **Never store cryovials submerged in the liquid phase of LN₂** unless the
  manufacturer approves it — trapped LN₂ can cause the vial to explode on warming.
  Use **vapour-phase** storage.

## 4. Handling & Temperature Excursions

- Record the exact storage location (unit, rack, box, position) in **BioLIMS v4**
  when you store a sample, so others can find it.
- If a freezer **alarm sounds or an excursion is suspected**: move samples to the
  designated backup unit, **log the excursion in BioLIMS v4**, and **raise a
  discrepancy/incident** so affected samples can be assessed.
- Ultra-low freezers and cryo-tanks are on **24/7 temperature monitoring** with
  remote alarms; do not silence an alarm without resolving the cause.

## 5. Good Practice

- Keep **duplicate copies of critical samples in a separate storage unit/location**.
- Use **cryo-resistant labels** below 0 °C — standard labels detach in the freezer.
- Never overfill a freezer; airflow keeps temperature uniform.
