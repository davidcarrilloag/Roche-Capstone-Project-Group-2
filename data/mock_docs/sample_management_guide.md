# Sample Management Guide

> **Document owner:** Sample Management Team
> **Version:** 3.0 — Internal use only (mock document for demo purposes)

This guide explains how to check, order, store, and report on laboratory samples
using the **Sample Management System (SMS)**.

---

## 1. Checking Current Sample Stock Levels

Stock levels are tracked in the **Sample Management System (SMS)**.

1. Open the **Sample Management System (SMS)** from your Virtual Workspace.
2. Go to **Inventory → Stock Overview**.
3. Filter by sample type, project, or storage location.
4. The **Available** column shows the quantity currently in stock; the
   **Reserved** column shows quantities already allocated to other experiments.
5. Items highlighted in **red** are below the minimum stock threshold and should
   be reordered.

> To check stock for a specific sample, use the search bar at the top of the
> Stock Overview and enter the sample ID or name.

---

## 2. Placing a Sample Order

1. In SMS, go to **Orders → New Order**.
2. Search for the sample you need and click **Add to order**.
3. Enter the required quantity and the project code to charge.
4. Select the **required-by date**. Allow extra lead time for cold-chain items.
5. Review the order summary and click **Submit Order**.
6. Orders above your approval limit are routed to your line manager
   automatically.

You can track order status under **Orders → My Orders**. Statuses are:
*Submitted → Approved → In Transit → Delivered.*

---

## 3. Labelling and Storing Samples Correctly

### Labelling
- Every sample must carry an **SMS-generated barcode label**. Print it from
  **Inventory → Print Label**.
- Labels must include: sample ID, sample type, date, and owner initials.
- Use **cryo-resistant labels** for anything stored below 0 °C — standard labels
  detach in the freezer.

### Storage
- Store each sample at the temperature specified on its label and in SMS.
- Record the exact storage location (freezer, rack, box, position) in SMS when
  you put the sample away, so others can find it.
- Never store incompatible sample types in the same box.

---

## 4. Reporting a Sample Discrepancy

Report a discrepancy whenever the physical stock does not match SMS (for
example a missing vial, a wrong label, or an unexpected quantity).

1. In SMS, open the sample record.
2. Click **Report Discrepancy**.
3. Choose the discrepancy type: *Missing*, *Surplus*, *Mislabelled*, or
   *Damaged*.
4. Describe what you observed and, if possible, attach a photo.
5. Submit. The Sample Management Team investigates and updates the record.

> Do **not** adjust stock counts manually. Always raise a discrepancy so the
> change is traceable.

## 5. Cold Chain and Temperature Requirements

Maintaining the cold chain is critical for sample integrity.

| Storage class | Temperature | Typical use |
|---------------|-------------|-------------|
| Room temperature | 15–25 °C | Stable reagents |
| Refrigerated | 2–8 °C | Many biological reagents |
| Frozen | −20 °C | Short-term sample storage |
| Ultra-low | −80 °C | Long-term biological storage |
| Cryogenic | ≤ −150 °C (liquid nitrogen) | Cell lines, critical samples |

Rules:
- Move cold-chain samples quickly; do not leave them at room temperature longer
  than the time stated on the sample record.
- Use a **pre-cooled transport container** when moving frozen samples between
  labs.
- If a freezer alarm sounds or a temperature excursion is suspected, move the
  samples to a backup unit and **raise a discrepancy / incident immediately**.
- Record any temperature excursion in SMS so affected samples can be assessed.
