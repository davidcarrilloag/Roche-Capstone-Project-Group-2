# Roche Scientist Onboarding Guide

> **Audience:** New scientists joining a Roche research or QC laboratory.
> **Document owner:** Lab IT & Onboarding Team
> **Version:** 1.4 — Internal use only (mock document for demo purposes)

Welcome to Roche! This guide walks you through everything you need to do during
your first days in the lab. Follow the sections in order.

---

## 1. Requesting Access to Internal Systems

All system access is managed through the **Roche Identity Portal** (RIP).

1. Open the Roche Identity Portal from your browser bookmark or the intranet
   homepage.
2. Sign in with your Roche corporate credentials (the ones from your welcome
   email).
3. Go to **My Access → Request New Access**.
4. Search for the system you need (for example *Sample Management System*,
   *LIMS*, or *Electronic Lab Notebook*).
5. Select the role that matches your job description. If unsure, choose
   **Standard Scientist** — your manager can extend it later.
6. Add a short business justification (e.g. "Required for daily QC testing").
7. Submit. Your line manager receives an approval request automatically.

**Typical approval time:** 1–2 business days. You will receive a confirmation
in the Identity Portal once access is granted.

### Requesting access to the Sample Management System specifically
To request access to the **Sample Management System (SMS)**:
1. In the Roche Identity Portal, search for **"Sample Management System"**.
2. Select the **SMS – Scientist** role.
3. In the justification, include your lab code and the type of samples you
   handle.
4. Submit. Approval is granted by the Sample Management key user, not your line
   manager, so allow up to 3 business days.

## 2. Uploading Your Required Onboarding Documents

Before you can be granted lab access, you must upload your signed onboarding
documents.

1. Open the **Onboarding Hub** from the intranet.
2. Go to **My Tasks → Documents to Submit**.
3. Upload the following as PDF:
   - Signed confidentiality agreement
   - Lab safety training certificate
   - Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP) acknowledgement (QC labs only)
4. Each document is reviewed within 24 hours. A green checkmark means it is
   accepted.

If a document is rejected, you will receive a comment explaining why. Re-upload
the corrected version in the same task.

## 3. Setting Up Your Virtual Login Device

Many lab applications run inside a **virtual session** (a remote desktop) so you
can move between lab workstations without losing your work.

1. On any lab workstation, open the **Roche Virtual Workspace** client from the
   desktop.
2. Sign in with your corporate credentials.
3. The first time, you will be asked to register the device for
   **multi-factor authentication (MFA)**. Use the Roche Authenticator app on
   your phone.
4. Your virtual session now follows you between devices — log in on any lab
   workstation and your applications resume where you left off.

**Tip:** Always **disconnect** (not log off) when you step away. Disconnecting
keeps your session running so it resumes instantly on the next device.

## 4. Applications You Need on Day One

| Application | Purpose | Where to find it |
|-------------|---------|------------------|
| Electronic Lab Notebook (ELN) | Record experiments | Virtual Workspace |
| Sample Management System (SMS) | Order & track samples | Virtual Workspace |
| LIMS | Test results & QC data | Virtual Workspace |
| Roche Identity Portal | Manage your access | Browser bookmark |
| Roche Authenticator | MFA for secure logins | Phone app store (corporate) |

All virtual applications appear automatically in your Virtual Workspace once
your access requests are approved.

## 5. Who to Contact for IT Issues

- **Password resets & login problems:** Roche IT Service Desk, available 24/7
  via the **Service Portal** or by phone at the number on your badge holder.
- **Application errors or crashes:** Raise an incident through the assistant or
  the Service Portal. Include the application name and what you were doing.
- **Lab instrument connectivity:** Contact your **Local Lab IT key user** (the
  name is posted on the noticeboard in each lab).

> If your virtual session keeps crashing, raise an IT incident and include how
> often it happens and which application was open at the time.

---

## 6. Booking Lab Instruments for the First Time

Shared instruments (HPLC, centrifuges, microscopes) are booked through the
**Instrument Booking System**.

1. Open the **Instrument Booking System** from the Virtual Workspace.
2. Select your lab location from the dropdown.
3. Choose the instrument and an available time slot.
4. Add the purpose of your booking (e.g. "Sample prep — project X").
5. Confirm. You will receive a reminder 15 minutes before your slot.

**First-time users** must complete the instrument-specific training before the
system lets them book. If an instrument is greyed out, your training record is
not yet complete — contact your lab supervisor.
