# Standard Operating Procedure: Cleaning of Laboratory Equipment

> **SOP ID:** LAB-CLN-002
> **Document owner:** Lab Operations / EHS
> **Version:** 2.1 — Internal use only (mock document for demo purposes)
> **Review cycle:** Annual

This SOP describes how to clean and maintain common laboratory equipment safely
and consistently. Follow it exactly. When in doubt, stop and ask your
supervisor.

---

## 1. General Cleaning Rules and Safety Precautions

- Always wear appropriate **personal protective equipment (PPE)**: gloves, lab
  coat, and safety glasses.
- Ensure the equipment is **powered off and unplugged** before cleaning, unless
  the procedure states otherwise.
- **Never spray** liquids directly onto electronic equipment. Apply cleaning
  agent to a wipe or cloth first.
- Use **lint-free wipes** for optical and electronic surfaces.
- Dispose of used wipes and gloves in the correct waste stream.
- Allow surfaces to **air-dry** fully before powering equipment back on.
- Record completed cleaning in the **equipment logbook**.

**Recommended general disinfectant:** 70% isopropanol (IPA), unless a specific
incompatibility is noted below.

---

## 2. Centrifuge Cleaning Procedure

Clean the centrifuge after every use and perform a deep clean weekly.

1. Power off and unplug the centrifuge. Wait until the rotor has fully stopped.
2. Remove the rotor and buckets.
3. Wipe the rotor chamber with a cloth dampened with **70% isopropanol**.
4. For spills, clean immediately with 70% isopropanol; for biological spills
   follow the decontamination procedure in Section 6 first.
5. Inspect the rotor for cracks or corrosion. Report any damage — do not use a
   damaged rotor.
6. Wipe the rotor and buckets with 70% isopropanol and let them air-dry.
7. Reassemble once everything is completely dry.
8. Leave the lid open for 10 minutes to let residual vapour evaporate.

> **HP centrifuge note:** For HP-branded centrifuges, use 70% isopropanol on the
> chamber and rotor only. Do **not** apply solvents to the control touchscreen —
> clean the screen as described in Section 5.

---

## 3. Pipette Cleaning and Maintenance

1. Wipe the external body with a cloth dampened with 70% isopropanol.
2. For the lower shaft, unscrew it according to the manufacturer's instructions
   and wipe with 70% isopropanol.
3. Never immerse a pipette in liquid.
4. Check the seal and O-ring for wear; replace if pipetting becomes inaccurate.
5. Recalibrate pipettes every 6 months, or immediately after any drop or
   suspected damage.
6. Store pipettes upright in the pipette stand.

---

## 4. Microscope Cleaning Procedure

1. Blow loose dust off lenses with a rubber blower — never wipe dry dust, as it
   scratches the coating.
2. Clean objective and eyepiece lenses with **lens tissue** lightly moistened
   with **lens cleaning solution** (not isopropanol, which can damage lens
   cement on some objectives).
3. Wipe the stage and body with a cloth dampened with 70% isopropanol.
4. Cover the microscope with its dust cover when not in use.
5. Never touch lens surfaces with bare fingers.

---

## 5. HP Laptop and Touchscreen Device Cleaning

Lab HP laptops and touchscreen devices need regular disinfection because they
are touched with gloves throughout the day.

1. Power off the device and unplug it from power.
2. Use **pre-moistened 70% isopropanol wipes**, or apply 70% isopropanol to a
   soft lint-free cloth.
3. **Never spray** liquid directly onto the screen, keyboard, or ports — spraying
   can force liquid inside and damage the device.
4. Gently wipe the screen in one direction; wipe the keyboard and palm rest.
5. Avoid excess moisture around ports and hinges.
6. Let the device air-dry completely (at least 5 minutes) before powering on.
7. Do not use bleach, acetone, or abrasive cleaners on any HP device.

---

## 6. Decontamination After Biological Samples

Perform this procedure before standard cleaning whenever equipment has been in
contact with biological samples.

1. Put on fresh gloves and any additional PPE required by the sample's risk
   level.
2. Apply an approved **disinfectant** (e.g. a 1:10 freshly prepared bleach
   solution, or an approved quaternary-ammonium product) to the contaminated
   surface.
3. Observe the required **contact time** stated on the disinfectant label
   (typically 10 minutes).
4. Wipe away the disinfectant residue with water, then finish with 70%
   isopropanol.
5. Dispose of all materials as **biohazard waste**.
6. Record the decontamination in the logbook, including the disinfectant used.

> Note: Bleach is corrosive to metal. After the required contact time, always
> remove bleach residue and finish with isopropanol to protect equipment.

---

## 7. Cleaning Frequency Recommendations

| Equipment | Routine clean | Deep clean |
|-----------|---------------|------------|
| Centrifuge | After each use | Weekly |
| Pipettes | Daily (exterior) | Monthly (full service) |
| Microscope | After each session | Monthly |
| HP laptop / touchscreen | Daily | Weekly |
| Shared benches | After each use | Daily |
