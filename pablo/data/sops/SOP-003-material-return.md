# Material Return Procedures via Automated Conveyor Network

## 1. Pre-Return Material Classification & Preparation

Before dispatching any laboratory equipment, chemical reagents, or biological
samples back to the central inventory depot, prepare the materials according to
their hazard and handling classification:

- **Hazardous and volatile compounds:** any toxic, highly reactive, or odorous
  chemicals originally delivered in dedicated containment shells **MUST** be
  packed inside those identical secondary outer shells before transit.
- **Liquid chemicals and biological assets:** place chemical containers and life
  science reagents securely inside a **gray technical containment capsule** and
  ensure the locking ring is fully sealed.
- **Glassware and general consumables:** no specialized containment preparation
  is required. Clean glassware and unused consumables may be placed directly into
  the standardized transit crates.

## 2. Crate Loading and Weight Restrictions

- **Capacity limits:** a maximum of **three** gray technical capsules may be
  loaded into a single transit crate.
- **Stacking rules:** do not stack containment units vertically. Contents must
  **NOT** protrude past the top rim of the transit crate.
- **Weight threshold:** the absolute maximum allowable weight for any single
  transit crate is **20 kg**. Overweight crates are automatically rejected by the
  conveyor's inline scales.
- **Empty crate returns:** when returning empty logistics units, a maximum of
  three crates may be nested together. No items are permitted inside nested
  returns; the lower units must be entirely free of debris.
- **Identification tagging:** every returned crate must feature a highly visible
  adhesive routing label indicating the sender's full name, cost center, and
  originating laboratory bay room number.

## 3. Automated Routing and Operational Windows

- **Life science matrices & biologicals:** route exclusively to the
  **First Floor Cryo-Depot**.
- **Glassware, tools, and ambient chemicals:** route to the **Ground Floor
  Central Inventory Station**.
- **System availability hours:** the automated return conveyor accepts inbound
  traffic Monday–Friday strictly between **08:15 and 16:30**. Do not load the
  system outside these hours.

## 4. Notification Requirement

Immediately after pressing "Send" on the conveyor terminal console, log the
transit tracking ID or send a brief digital notification to the Logistics
Operations Desk via the **LabLend & Go** application or by emailing
`supplyhub@logistics.com`.
