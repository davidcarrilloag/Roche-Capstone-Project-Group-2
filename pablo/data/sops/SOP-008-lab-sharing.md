# Collaborative Lab Sharing and Digital Workflows

## 1. The Core Philosophy of Shared Lab Environments

In the Building in Basel, laboratory footprints are designed around an
**open-concept architecture** rather than isolated, single-team rooms. These
expansive laboratory spaces are used by larger, cross-functional scientific teams
working in parallel on diverse experiments within the same facility.

- **Shared infrastructure:** core instruments, specialized workbenches, and heavy
  analytical equipment are shared across multiple project teams.
- **Agile adaptability:** this decentralized approach scales space optimization so
  that high-demand resources are never underutilized.

## 2. Digital Lab Operations: The Smart Laboratory Suite (SLS)

The campus relies on the **Smart Laboratory Suite (SLS)**, a unified digital
platform for planning and executing daily laboratory tasks:

- **Equipment booking & reservations:** all shared high-value instruments must be
  reserved in advance through the SLS scheduling module to prevent conflicts.
- **Material tracking & ordering:** the suite tracks consumable inventory, reagent
  levels, and material lot numbers in real time.
- **Experiment lifecycle planning:** researchers map experimental timelines
  digitally, linking required bench space and equipment to upcoming protocols.

## 3. Laboratory Etiquette and Cultural Mindset

Every researcher is expected to adhere to the core principles of the **Lab
Etiquette Guidelines**:

- **Proactive planning:** experiment planning is the top operational priority.
  Scientists must use SLS to book instruments, volatile storage cabinets, and
  specialized reagents well in advance, so everything is pre-staged when the
  experiment begins.
- **Transparency and fairness:** equipment slots must be booked accurately —
  holding slots as "buffers" without active experiments is strictly prohibited.
- **Aesthetic and operational cleanliness:** shared workbenches must be completely
  cleared, sanitized, and reset to their default baseline configuration at the end
  of every shift so the next researcher can begin immediately.
